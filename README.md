# 📊 HR Attrition Analytics Dashboard

## 📝 Project Overview
This project provides a comprehensive analysis of **Employee Attrition** within an organization. By utilizing data visualization and exploratory data analysis (EDA), this dashboard identifies key factors contributing to employee turnover, such as salary slabs, age groups, and job roles. It is designed to help HR departments make data-driven decisions to improve employee retention.

---

## 🚀 Key Insights & Findings
The dashboard reveals several critical trends regarding why employees leave the company:

* **Overall Metrics**: The organization has a total of **1,470 employees** with an **attrition rate of 16%** (238 employees).
* **Salary Impact**: A significant **68.49%** of attrition occurs among employees in the lowest salary tier (**Upto 5k**), suggesting a strong correlation between low pay and turnover.
* **Age Demographics**: The **26-35 age group** experiences the highest attrition with **116 departures**, followed by the 18-25 group.
* **Departmental Trends**: The **Research & Development** and **Sales** departments show higher activity, with 'Research Scientists' and 'Sales Representatives' being the most affected roles.
* **Education Background**: Employees with a background in **Life Sciences** (89) and **Medical** (63) fields represent the majority of those leaving the firm.
* **Work-Life Factors**: The average monthly salary across the workforce is **6.50K**, and the average employee age is approximately **37 years**.

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
* **Power BI**: Used for creating the interactive dashboard and data visualizations.
* **Power Query**: Utilized for automated data cleaning, transformation, and shaping.
* **DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)**: Employed to create custom measures and calculated columns for deep-dive analysis.

---

## 📖 How to Navigate the Dashboard
To get the most out of this analytics tool, follow these steps:

1.  **Department Filters**: Use the top slicers to filter data specifically for **Human Resources**, **Research & Development**, or **Sales**.
2.  **Attrition Analysis**: Observe the **Attrition by SalarySlab** donut chart to see how compensation affects retention.
3.  **Role Comparison**: Check the **Job Attrition Vs Satisfaction Rate** matrix to identify which specific roles are reporting low satisfaction scores.
4.  **Tenure Check**: Review the **Attrition by YearsAtCompany** area chart to see if employees are leaving early in their careers or after long-term service.
